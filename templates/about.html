About Speculum

Digital preservation through local mirroring

The Project

Speculum is a digital archiving initiative dedicated to preserving online content at risk of disappearing. We mirror websites, YouTube channels, and digital publications for future generations.

The name "Speculum" (mirror in Latin) reflects our mission: creating faithful reflections of the web before it vanishes. Every site we archive becomes a permanent record of digital culture.

What We Archive

  • Personal websites and blogs
  • Independent publications and zines
  • YouTube channels and video content
  • Community forums and archives
  • Cultural documentation
  • Sites at risk of closure

Technology

Speculum uses open source tools for archiving:

  • wget — Complete website mirroring
  • yt-dlp — YouTube video archiving
  • SingleFile — JavaScript-heavy sites
  • Ollama — AI-powered categorization

Contribute

You can help preserve the web:

  • Submit sites at risk through our request form
  • Spread the word in your community

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Speculum?
Speculum is a digital archiving initiative dedicated to preserving online content at risk of disappearing. We mirror websites, YouTube channels, and digital publications for future generations.
What types of content does Speculum archive?
We archive personal websites and blogs, independent publications and zines, YouTube channels and video content, community forums, cultural documentation, and sites at risk of closure.
What technology does Speculum use?
Speculum uses open source tools including wget for complete website mirroring, yt-dlp for YouTube video archiving, SingleFile for JavaScript-heavy sites, and Ollama for AI-powered categorization.
How can I request a site to be archived?
You can submit sites at risk through our request form. Simply provide the URL and optionally a description of why the site should be preserved.
Is Speculum free to use?
Yes, Speculum is completely free. All archived content is publicly accessible for research, education, and preservation purposes.
